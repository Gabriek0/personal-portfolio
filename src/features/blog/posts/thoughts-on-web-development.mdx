The web development landscape changes fast. New frameworks, build tools, and paradigms emerge every year. Here are some thoughts on what stays constant amid the noise.

## The Tools Keep Changing

From jQuery to React, from Webpack to Turbopack, the tooling evolves. What matters is understanding the fundamentals: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Everything else builds on top of that foundation. Learn the core well, and new tools become easier to pick up.

## Performance Still Matters

Users expect instant load times. Core Web Vitals aren't just metrics—they affect how people experience your work. Lazy loading, code splitting, and thoughtful asset management remain essential, regardless of which framework you choose.

## Developer Experience

Good DX isn't a luxury. Hot reloading, type safety, and clear error messages make development faster and less frustrating. Invest in your setup; it pays off over the life of a project.

## Ship It

The best portfolio is one that exists. Perfect is the enemy of done. Start simple, iterate, and share your work. The rest will follow.
