title: Learning by Doing
description: Why hands-on projects beat passive learning when it comes to mastering new skills.
date: 2026-01-20
---

There's a gap between knowing something and being able to do it. Reading tutorials and watching videos builds familiarity, but real competence comes from building things.

## The Tutorial Trap

It's easy to follow along and feel like you're learning. The code works, the concepts seem clear. Then you close the tab and try to build something from scratch—and suddenly nothing works. That's because following isn't the same as creating.

## Build Something Small

Start with a tiny project. A to-do app, a simple API, a personal site. It doesn't need to be original or impressive. The goal is to make decisions, hit errors, and solve them yourself. That's where the real learning happens.

## Embrace the Struggle

When you're stuck, you're learning. Debugging, searching, and eventually figuring it out—that process etches the knowledge into your brain. The projects that frustrate you the most often teach you the most.

## Keep Shipping

Every finished project, no matter how small, is proof that you can build. Over time, that proof compounds. So pick something, build it, and ship it.
